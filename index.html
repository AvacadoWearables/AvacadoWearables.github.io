Why We Do It

About

     The Wearable Technology industry is predicted to generate 53 billion in hardware revenue by 2019, with roughly​ 1 in 5 Americans already using a wearable device. Current players in the industry allow users to monitor health by tracking physical activity. However, physical activity isn’t the only contributor to health; in fact, overall health is largely impacted by emotional health. As of 2016, 90% of doctor visits have been caused by stress-related illnesses. In a billion-dollar industry, there is a clear need for a product that understands the important role of mental health in overall physical well-being.

     We're addressing this huge industry oversight with AvoCado, a hardware/software system that tracks both physical activity and mental health. Cado, the wearable device, tracks physical activity and heart rate. These metrics are then sent to Avo , the companion software application. Avo uniquely tracks emotional health through the user’s generated online data (social media feeds, emails, calendar, etc.) to perform textual sentiment analysis on. Coupling the results with received physical data indicative of stress (irregular heartbeat), Avo is able to pinpoint, analyze and predict stressful events.

Feel Better.

For all users, stress/heart monitoring and coaching works as a preventative measure to avoid the progression or acquisition of new health issues. With the average American spending over $1,300 annually on their health deductible, users will save time and money spent on emergency room visits, checkups, and medication.

For users with stress-triggered health conditions, such as Atrial fibrillation, Cado provides a unique opportunity to more accurately predict attacks. Atrial fibrillation patients, for example, must closely manage their stress levels, as high-stress and high-energy contexts can trigger a stroke. The Cado’s stress prevention functionality will provide a situationally-informed warning in such contexts, giving the user time to find a calmer environment.

Save Money.

Stress-related health issues result on average of $3K in healthcare expenses per person a year. Stress prevention and management provided by our products can help the user save hundreds, even thousands of dollars a year in addition to extending longevity.

Be Less Stressed.

We're providing a never-before commercially attempted health and activity tracker that has a direct concentration in emotional health. In addition to physical data tracked by most wearables, Avo takes advantage of data generated by the user’s online presence to provide an informed analysis of user data. With regards to stress prevention, Avo takes a two-step approach. Firstly, the application notifies the user when he/she is becoming stressed. While the average person might assume a user would be aware of this, research shows that this is not typically the case. Even if the user is aware of stress, it has been shown that an objective third-party directly addressing the issue will help resolve the issue. For this same reason, Avo ’s guidance capabilities, suggesting solutions for stressful events, also provide the user with a path to a calmer life.
